Disaster strikes Bolt's final race

In the 2017 world championships held in Great Britian the world again awaited a legendary performance by the great Usain Bolt. It came time for the 4 x 100 meter relay and bolt was running anchor. When he recieved the baton his team was a few strides ahead of the second place team but then disaster struck. Just a few seconds into his race, Bolt tore a tendon in his left hamsting causing him to collape on the track. It was truly a sad sight to see a world renowned runner go out like this in his last race. Bolt continues to be admired as one of the greatest athletes of all time but seeing this race was heartbreaking.

Post runnning ventures pro soccer

After his running career, bolt pursued professional soccer, apparently trying to fulfill a childhood dream. He played for the central coast mariners in Australia. His speed was challenging for defenders to counter and bolt even scored 2 goals in his short career. Bolt eventually left the team saying it just wasn't right for him but that it was fun while it lasted.

Settling down What is he doing now?

Usian bolt still does track workouts with his old coach just to keep in shape but since his profesional careeer ended usian Bolt has more or less settled down. He now has three kids, an older daughter named Olympia Lightning bolt, and twins named Saint Leo Bolt and Thunder Bolt. He has dived into many other interests such as music and business ventures. Bolt is still keeping busy despite his reteirement from track and field.
